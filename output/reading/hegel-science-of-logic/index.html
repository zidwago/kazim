Overview

The Science of Logic is Hegel’s attempt to develop a presuppositionless philosophy — a logic that generates its own categories through the immanent movement of thought. I am reading the di Giovanni translation.

This is a long-running reading log, updated as I work through the text. I am not reading straight through but moving between sections as my understanding develops.

The Doctrine of Being

Being, Nothing, Becoming

The famous opening: pure being, without further determination, is the same as nothing. The first concrete thought is becoming — the unity of being and nothing in their passing over into each other.

What strikes me on re-reading is how much this depends on a very specific understanding of what “determination” means. Hegel’s claim is not that being and nothing are synonyms; it’s that without any determinate content, the thought of pure being collapses.
