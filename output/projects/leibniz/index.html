Leibniz

active 16/04/2025

Continuing engagement with Leibniz's primary texts and secondary literature, developing original interpretive work on incommensurables and modal metaphysics.

Background

I have read the majority of the texts found in Philosophical Texts (Oxford) and Philosophical Essays (Hackett). I have discussed Leibniz’s metaphysics in a short general paper and written a longer paper on Leibniz’s use of incommensurables as it applies to his metaphysics.

Secondary Literature Engaged

  • “Leibniz’s Modal Metaphysics” — read closely
  • “Leibniz’s Exoteric Philosophy” — read closely
  • Nicholas Jolley, Leibniz (Routledge) — currently working through
  • The Oxford Handbook of Leibniz — currently working through

Current Directions

I am looking to develop a new line of research going deeper into the Leibniz literature. The incommensurables thread is potentially something I might explore further, but I want to see what emerges from the secondary literature I’m currently engaging with.

A Spinoza pairing is also of interest — reading Leibniz and Spinoza together as interlocutors within the rationalist tradition, each pressing the other’s commitments in productive ways.
