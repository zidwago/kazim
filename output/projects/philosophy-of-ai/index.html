Philosophy of AI

active 16/04/2025

Surveying the philosophy of artificial intelligence literature and working through foundational papers, with an eye toward developing a philosophical perspective on large language models.

Current State

This project is in its earliest stages. I am surveying the field — reading broadly to understand the landscape before committing to a specific line of inquiry.

My entry point is the question of what LLMs are doing when they produce language. This is not primarily an ethics question (though ethics is never fully separable), but a question about reasoning, meaning, and the normative structure of language use.

Reading Pipeline

Papers I have scanned and will be exploring further:

  • Vincent C. Müller, “Philosophy of AI: A Structured Overview” (Cambridge Handbook on the Law, Ethics and Policy of AI)
  • Vincent C. Müller, “Ethics of Artificial Intelligence and Robotics” (Stanford Encyclopedia)
  • Adam Pease, “Ontology and Information Systems” (Stanford Encyclopedia)
  • “Philosophy of Technology” (Stanford Encyclopedia)
  • Dario Amodei, “The Adolescence of Technology”
  • Mirco Sambrotta, “LLMs and the Logical Space of Reasons” — particularly promising; seems to connect AI with the Pittsburgh school (Sellars, Brandom)

Open Directions

I don’t yet know where this project will lead. Some possibilities:

  • A close reading of Sambrotta’s paper, developing or critiquing his use of Brandom
  • An essay on what Leibniz’s concept of expression might offer the philosophy of AI
  • A more general piece on why philosophy of language (rather than philosophy of mind) might be the right framework for thinking about LLMs
