## The Mathematical Context

Leibniz's engagement with incommensurables — quantities that cannot be expressed as ratios of whole numbers — runs throughout his mathematical career. The question is what philosophical weight he places on them.

## Metaphysical Implications

The relationship between the mathematical and the metaphysical in Leibniz is never straightforward. His monadology demands a certain kind of completeness, and yet his mathematics constantly gestures toward the incomplete, the irrational, the incommensurable.
