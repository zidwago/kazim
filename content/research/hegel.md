## Background

I have done a great deal of reading on Hegel. I have surveyed much of the secondary literature in the English language and read a good deal of the *Science of Logic* and some of the *Phenomenology of Spirit*. I have much further to go.

## Secondary Literature Engaged

Texts I have engaged with — some very deeply and completely, others less so:

- Paul Redding, *Conceptual Harmonies: The Origins and Relevance of Hegel's Logic*
- Georg Lukács, *The Young Hegel: Studies in the Relations between Dialectics and Economics*
- Frederick Beiser, *Hegel* (2005)
- Charles Taylor, *Hegel* (1975)
- Terry Pinkard, *German Philosophy 1760–1860: The Legacy of Idealism*
- Various articles and chapters

## Current Reading

- Frederick Beiser's Routledge text on Hegel — want to engage more deeply
- Patrick Gardiner (ed.), *19th Century Philosophy* — enjoying working through this collection
- Hegel's *Science of Logic* — ongoing close reading

## Planned Expansions

- **Kant**: I want to read the *Prolegomena* and Sebastian Gardner's Routledge guide on Kant and the *Critique of Pure Reason*. Strengthening my Kant is important for deepening the Hegel work.
- **Marx**: Expanding into Marx and the broader 19th-century context is a natural next step from the Hegel reading.
- **More primary texts**: The *Phenomenology* demands a more sustained engagement than I've given it so far.
